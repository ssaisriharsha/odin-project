Vada

Description

Vada is a popular South Indian savory snack known for its crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior. It is typically made from a batter consisting of soaked and ground urad dal (split black lentils), seasoned with spices like black pepper, cumin seeds, and curry leaves. The batter is shaped into rounds or doughnut-like shapes and deep-fried until golden brown and crunchy. Vadas are often enjoyed hot and served with coconut chutney or sambar, a flavorful lentil-based stew. They are a favorite street food and are also served during festivals and special occasions across India. Vadas are valued for their delicious taste, contrasting textures, and ability to satisfy as a satisfying snack or part of a larger meal.

Ingredients

  • Cumin seeds
  • Urad dal
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Water
