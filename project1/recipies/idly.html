Idly

Description

Idli is a traditional South Indian steamed rice cake known for its soft, spongy texture and mild flavor. It is a popular breakfast dish enjoyed across India and beyond. Idli batter is made from a combination of soaked and ground rice and urad dal (split black lentils), which is then fermented overnight. This fermentation process lends a slight tanginess to the batter and enhances its digestibility. To prepare idlis, the batter is poured into special idli molds and steamed until cooked through. The result is a round, fluffy cake that is typically served hot with sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and coconut chutney. Idlis are appreciated for their lightness and versatility, often enjoyed not only for breakfast but also as a snack or light meal throughout the day. They are a staple in South Indian cuisine and are valued for their nutritious qualities, being gluten-free and low in fat.

Ingredients

  • Parboiled rice
  • Urad dal
  • Fenugreek seeds
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Fermentation
